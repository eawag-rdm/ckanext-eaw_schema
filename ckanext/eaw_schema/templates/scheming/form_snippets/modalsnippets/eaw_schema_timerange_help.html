Timerange

Denote the timerange(s) to which the data in the package relates, i.e. when the state of the system under consideration was examined. Usually that would be the time(s) at which field-measurements were made or samples were taken.

Enter " * " if no specific time-range can be associated with the package's data.

All times are UTC.

Pad single digit days or months with a leading zero,
e.g. "7" → "07".

Points in time and implicit time-ranges:

field-value meaning
2015 Jan 1, 2015 to Dec 31, 2015
2015-03 March 2015
2015-03-11 March 11, 2015
2015-03-11T23 March 3, 2015, [23:00 .. 24:00)
* eternity

Use "TO" to express explicit time-ranges:

field-value meaning
2014 TO 2016 2014, 2015 and 2016
1969-06 TO 1969-08 the summer of 1969
*  TO 1848-09-12 anytime before the existence of Switzerland
1991-06-15 TO  * anytime after a Mount Pinatubo eruption

Use multiple time-ranges to express disjunct periods:

field field-value meaning
Timerange 1 2013-06 TO 2013-08 Measurements were taken during
summers 2013 and 2014.
Timerange 2 2014-06 TO 2014-08

Ignore superflous "Timerange <N>" fields. Empty ones will be discarded.
