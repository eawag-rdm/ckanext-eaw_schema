{# Help text for timerange field for package create/update #}

Timerange

Denote the timerange(s) to which the data in the package relates, i.e. when the state of the system under consideration was examined. Usually that would be the time(s) at which field-measurements were made or samples were taken. The date when you analyzed the samples in the lab is likely irrelevant in this context.

All times are UTC.
Pad single digit days or months with a leading zero, e.g. "7" ➙ "07".

Points in time and implicit time-ranges:
2015Jan 1, 2015 to Dec 31, 2015
2015-03March 2015
2015-03-11March 11, 2015
2015-03-11T23March 3, 2015, [23:00 .. 24:00)
1969-10-29T22:30:04.765a particular millisecond
*eternity

Use "TO" to express explicit time-ranges:
2014 TO 20162014, 2015 and 2016
1969-06 TO 1969-08the summer of 1969
*  TO 1848-09-12anytime before the existence of Switzerland
1991-06-15 TO  *anytime after a Mount Pinatubo eruption

Use multiple time-ranges to express disjunct periods:
Timerange 12013-06 TO 2013-08Measurements were taken during
sommers 2013 and 2014.
Timerange 22014-06 TO 2014-08

This is a mandatory field.
Enter " * " if no specific time-range can be associated with the package's data.

Ignore superflous "Timerange <N>" fields. Empty ones will be discarded.
