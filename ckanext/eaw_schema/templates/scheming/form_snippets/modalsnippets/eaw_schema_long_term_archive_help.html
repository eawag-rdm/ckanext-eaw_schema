Long-term data package?

Indicates that you want to earmark this package for the long-term archive. A long-term data package is a data package that is considered important enough to be preserved for a time span on the order of a century. Long-term data packages are subject to special requirements regarding file formats, regarding the quality and completeness of the documentation and regarding the extent of their curation and peer-review.

There should be a department- or group-level policy in place to decide what data should go into the long-term archive.

Eawag guide to the publishing and archiving of research data
and follow in particular the sections on

  • File naming,
  • Folder structure and file-archives and
  • File formats.
